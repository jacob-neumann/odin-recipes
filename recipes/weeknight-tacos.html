Weeknight Tacos

Quick and Easy!

Tacos are a comfort food of mine. They are so flexible, and can be served with such a wide array of fixings! While making tacos al pastor, pollo asada, or carnitas might take too long for a weeknight dinner, there is an easier alternative. This recipe is one I use very often for a quick and easy dinner and meal prep! What takes this recipe from boring to spectacular is using homemade taco seasoning. I use modified version of this recipe 1 from Serious Eats and some habanero chilli powder to bump it up a notch. This recipe makes 3-4 servings,and can be easily scaled up as required.

I don't always have a lot of time or energy after work, so I appreciate that this recipe is fast! It only takes me about 20 minutes of prep before it is ready to serve, including making the taco seasoning from scratch! Even better, it doesn't generate a lot of dirty dishes. Now let's get to it!

Ingredients

  • 1lb ground beef 2
  • 1 can (15oz) kidney beans3
  • 1 can (15oz) black beans3
  • 1 batch of Serious Eats Taco Seasoning (modified) or 1 bag of pre-made mix1,4
  • 1-2 tbsp lime juice (optional)5
  • Tortilla Chips or Tortillas to serve with the filling
  • Any desired toppings (cheese, lettuce, tomatoes, etc.)

Necessary Equipment

  • 1 large skillet or frying pan
  • 1 spatula to stir
  • Small bowl to mix taco seasoning
  • Measuring spoons to make taco seasoning

Directions

  1. Make taco seasoning4 using this Serious Eats recipe with the modifications in note 1 (if desired)
  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, brown beef and break into small chunks with the spatula
  3. While cooking the meat, open the cans of beans (don't dump out the liquid!)
  4. Once the beef is cooked, add both cans of beans, taco seasoning, and lime juice. Stir until combined and allow to come to a boil
  5. Reduce heat to low and stir as needed. Allow liquid to thicken until it reaches your desired consistency
  6. Remove from heat and serve with tortilla chips or tortillas and desired toppings

Notes

  1. I make a couple of minor modifications to the Serious Eats taco seasoning. I double the cumin and garlic powder, and replace a third of the chilli powder with habanero chilli powder from Savory Spice®
  2. I use 88% lean ground beef from Costco. I would avoid using beef with too much more fat so as to prevent significant splatter when cooking.
  3. Pinto beans are also good! Feel free to use only your favorite bean rather than having two different types as I do.
  4. Using pre-made taco seasoning is fine in a pinch.
  5. The lime juice is strictly optional. In fact, I didn't start adding it until recently. The acid from the lime juice does add some delicious contrast and flair to the tacos, but it works fine without it.
